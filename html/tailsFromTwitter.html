A love Story

This essay was so hard to write, but not in the usual ways. I was angry about it, angry at myself, angry at the reader. It grew from that. https://t.co/ADmID46sEV

— Blair Braverman (@BlairBraverman) December 29, 2016

I knew I could easily sell an essay abt having a trans partner and that pissed me off in general. My partner's body is not my personal essay

— Blair Braverman (@BlairBraverman) December 29, 2016

I was trying to promote Ice Cube without writing about the north specifically. Q was the one who finally convinced me to write this.

— Blair Braverman (@BlairBraverman) December 29, 2016

But I hate the cliches of this genre. Deeply dehumanizing, self-congratulatory. I was disgusted with myself for even considering.

— Blair Braverman (@BlairBraverman) December 29, 2016

I decided to write something subversive, twisting people's expectations. A clickbait headline and then railing at reader for clicking.

— Blair Braverman (@BlairBraverman) December 29, 2016

I wanted to make the reader as uncomfortable as I was.

— Blair Braverman (@BlairBraverman) December 29, 2016

I wanted to rail against the focus on trans people's bodies. And at the same time I was bitter at myself for participating, in whatever way.

— Blair Braverman (@BlairBraverman) December 29, 2016

For my first draft I started by describing my own body in deeply uncomfortable detail.

— Blair Braverman (@BlairBraverman) December 29, 2016

Like, you want to read about a body that's not your business? Here you go. Here's the only body I have a right to tell you about.

— Blair Braverman (@BlairBraverman) December 29, 2016

But then I started writing about Q. And I am so very, very in love with him.

— Blair Braverman (@BlairBraverman) December 29, 2016

And so the parts about Q were... a love story. So mismatched with the anger and bitterness I was framing it all with.

— Blair Braverman (@BlairBraverman) December 29, 2016

You want to know what it's like to have a trans partner? I can't tell you that. But I can tell you what it's like to love Q.

— Blair Braverman (@BlairBraverman) December 29, 2016

And frankly, his medical history has very little to do with any of that.

— Blair Braverman (@BlairBraverman) December 29, 2016

Someone commented that Q reads like a made-up cowboy fantasy figure (or something like that) and that cracked me the f up.

— Blair Braverman (@BlairBraverman) December 29, 2016

Like, you think you're gonna read about something exotic, but you're actually reading about a LITERAL COWBOY and horse thieves. Americana.

— Blair Braverman (@BlairBraverman) December 29, 2016

Another thing I wanted to confront queerness. I never felt like anything but straight until I started negotiating other people's reactions.

— Blair Braverman (@BlairBraverman) December 29, 2016

Now, this essay is one of my favorite things I've written. Because it holds that anger but grew to be about so much more than that.

— Blair Braverman (@BlairBraverman) December 29, 2016

And it feels... true. The deepest truth I'm capable of getting to. And the deepest love.

— Blair Braverman (@BlairBraverman) December 29, 2016

Also: BIG thx to @IsaacFitzgerald + @misscheuse for helping this take shape, as well as brilliant pals @helen_of_boyd @1demerith @sealln

— Blair Braverman (@BlairBraverman) December 29, 2016
